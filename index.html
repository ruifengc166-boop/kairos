Sample Readings

See what a timing reading looks like.

These samples show the level of clarity KAIROS aims to provide before asking for your own moment.

Love Sample
Question: They went silent for three days. I want to text tonight.
What KAIROS reads: recent silence + urgency + today’s window.
Main Signal: Wait.
Best Move: Wait 24 hours. If the feeling becomes calmer, ask one clear question.
Avoid: Do not send a paragraph tonight just to end the discomfort.
Career Sample
Question: I want to quit, but I also feel pressured and reactive.
What KAIROS reads: pressure is high; the move may be real, but the timing is not clean.
Main Signal: Prepare.
Best Move: Use the next three days to gather facts before making the move.
Avoid: Do not turn frustration into a permanent decision.
Wellbeing Sample
Question: I feel blocked. Should I push through or slow down?
What KAIROS reads: depletion + resistance + a short window for reset.
Main Signal: Rest.
Best Move: Simplify for 48 hours. Return to routine before forcing momentum.
Avoid: Do not treat exhaustion as a character flaw.
Decision Sample
Question: An opportunity appeared. Should I say yes this week?
What KAIROS reads: an opening is present, but the window needs one more clear signal.
Main Signal: Prepare first.
Best Move: Ask one clarifying question before saying yes.
Avoid: Do not confuse speed with alignment.
What You Receive

Personal Timing Signal.

A short reading designed to help you choose the cleanest next move.

  • Main Signal — Act, wait, prepare, speak clearly, rest, reset, or let go.
  • Timing Window — whether the current window is open, blocked, unstable, or still forming.
  • Why This Timing — the recent signal, emotional weather, and symbolic timing pattern.
  • Best Move — one clear next action.
  • What to Avoid — the move that would distort the timing.
  • Reflection Line — one sentence you can save and return to.
  • Optional Clear Window — a cleaner moment to act, if one appears.

KAIROS does not claim to predict fate. It reads timing.

The purpose is not to remove your judgment, but to help you see whether the moment is clean for action.
